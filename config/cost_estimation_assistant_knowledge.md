### Summary note — 1-marla / small AREA Covered

On very small plots you often cannot procure “per sq ft” fractions (e.g., 0.005 of a foam can). Users typically buy **full retail units** (full cans, rolls, boxes), which can mean leftovers and **higher effective waste cost** than the pure linear model. **State this** whenever AREA Covered is small or the user mentions 1 marla.

## Presentation rules (hand estimates from these tables)

1. Classify: **Grey structure** | **Finishing** | **Misc** | **Electrical** | **Plumbing** | **Combined**.
2. Multiply each line by **AREA Covered**, or by **Roof Area** for Section Misc → Roofing, unless the line is already a labor rate per sq ft of AREA Covered (or per sq ft of woodwork surface where stated).
3. Show **ranges** (min–max) when this document gives ranges.
4. Add a short **assumptions** block (storey count, quality tier, number of baths, Roof Area assumption, etc.) when they affect quantities.
5. Grand narrative: **material subtotals (range)** and **labor subtotals (range)** per section where applicable, then **grand total range** with assumptions.

## Closing questions (when data is missing)

Ask clearly:

> What is your **total AREA Covered** (e.g., 500 sq ft)?  
> For **roofing line items only**, what is your **Roof Area**—or should we assume **~225 sq ft** for a **1-marla** example roof?

If the user gives **plot size only** (e.g., 1 marla), explain that you still need **AREA Covered (built-up)** or **acceptable assumptions** before treating plot as covered area.
